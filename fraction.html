Fractions


A fraction is a number that describes a relationship between a part and a whole.

Real Life Example:


Like Fractions


Like fractions are the fractions which have the same denominators for example : 2/10 , 7/10 , 5/10 so on.

More examples:


Unlike Fractions


Unlike fractions are the fractions which DO NOT have the same denominators for example : 11/29 , 33/50 , 27/46 so on.

More examples:


Proper Fractions


Proper Fractions are the fractions in which the numerator is smaller than the denominator for example : 2/5 , 17/30 , 36/40 so on.

More examples:


Improper Fractions


Improper Fractions are the fractions in which the denominator is smaller than the numerator for example : 15/4 , 8/3 , 29/2 so on.

More examples:


Addition Of Fractions


Method 1: Butterfly


To do addition with the butterfly method follow these steps. Steps:

1.Write the two fractions beside each other, multiply the numerator of one fraction with the denominator of the other fraction and make a wing of the butterfly as shown.


2. Do the same with the numbers left. Then we get two answers, add both of them and we get the numerator for the answer.


3. To get the denominator for the answer multiply both of the denominators of the fractions and you have your answer.

Multiplication Of Fractions


Method 1: Pant - Shirt


To do multiplication with the pant-shirt method follow these steps. Steps:

1.Write the two fractions beside each other, see if the numerator of one fraction and the denominator of the other fraction come in one table.


2. If they do come in one table strike the numbers and write how many times it comes in the table as shown in the picture. But if they don't come in one table multiply the numerators and denominators directly


3. Once you do that multiply the two numerators and denominators together and you have your answer.

Division Of Fractions


Method 1: Keep , Flip and Change


To do division with the keep , flip and change method follow these steps. Steps:

1. Write the two fractions beside each other, again just like multiplication see if the numerator and denominator go in the same table and if they do strike then and write how many times it goes in the table.


2.If they go in the same table you must strike it. Now we will use keep , flip and change. Keep the first fraction as it is , flip the second fraction and change the division to multiplication. Multiply the fractions and you have your answer.
